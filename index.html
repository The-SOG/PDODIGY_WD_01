LINGARAJA

The temple’s origins are attributed to the Somavamsi dynasty, with later contributions from the Ganga rulers. Interestingly, the Lingaraja deity was originally found under a mango tree (known as Ekamra), which is why Bhubaneswar is often referred to as Ekamra Kshetra

  • Timings: Morning till evening
  • Nearest oldtown,BINDUSAGAR LAKE
  • Location: OLDTOWN

BHASKARESWAR

The temple was built by the Gangas during the 13th to 14th century CE, making it one of the oldest temples in Odisha. Its architecture stands out from other temples in Bhubaneswar. The temple is double-storied, with a unique two-tiered structure. The presiding deity is Lord Shiva, worshipped as Bhaskareswara. Notably, the temple faces west, and its most distinctive feature is a 9-foot-tall Shivalingam located in the sanctum sanctorum. This lingam is locally known as Vrihalingam. The exterior lacks intricate sculptues but features architectural motifs such as Pidha mundi and Khakhara mundi. Ganesha, Kartikeya, and Parvati are also present as Parsvadevtas.

  • Entry Fee: N/A
  • Timings:9:00 am to 6:00 pm
  • Nearest RAIL WAY
  • Location: BHUBANESWAR

RAJARANI TEMPLE

The Rajarani Temple is renowned for its intricate carvings and ornate figures. It’s a visual delight, with tiers of projections rising to form an impressive 18-meter-high tower. The temple is made primarily of yellow and red sandstone, which locals refer to as “Raja Rani.”

  • Entry Fee: N/A
  • Timings:6:00 am to 7:00 pm
  • Location: Bhubaneswar

ISKCON TEMPLE

Little do people know that Bhubaneswar’s ISKCON Temple holds the honour of being the largest in the world. Its foundation was laid in 1997 in remembrance of the birth anniversary of ISKCON founder, Shri Prabhupada. The shrine stands out for its neo-classical architectural style, the ethereal idols of Radha and Lord Krishna and the ornate arch at the entrance, among other things puts it among the divine places in Bhubaneswar to visit.
